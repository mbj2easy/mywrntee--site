MyWrntee · Warranty Tracker & Organizer

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add a warranty item?

Tap the + button on the Items tab or Home screen. You can either scan a receipt with your camera or enter the details manually. Fill in the product name, company, purchase date, and warranty period, then tap Save.

How do I scan a receipt?

When adding a new item, tap Scan Receipt at the top of the form. Point your camera at the receipt or choose a photo from your library. MyWrntee extracts the text using Live Text, then shows a Tap to Assign review screen where you tap each line to assign it to the correct field (store, date, price, etc.).

How do I organize items into groups?

Go to the Groups tab and tap + to create a new group. Give it a name, pick an icon and color. When adding or editing an item, select which group it belongs to. You can also create sub-groups for deeper organization (e.g., Home > Kitchen).

How do I enable iCloud Sync?

Go to Settings > iCloud and toggle on iCloud Sync. The app will need to restart to enable syncing. Once enabled, your warranties sync automatically across all your Apple devices signed into the same iCloud account.

How do I export my data?

Go to Settings > Data Management > Export Data. Choose your format:

  • CSV — Spreadsheet format, great for Excel or Numbers
  • PDF — Formatted report, ideal for insurance claims

How do I delete all my data?

Go to Settings > Data Management > Delete All Data. This will permanently remove all groups, items, and photos from the app. This action cannot be undone.

Is my data private?

Yes. All data is stored locally on your device. If you enable iCloud Sync, data is stored in your personal iCloud account. MyWrntee does not collect personal information, use analytics, or share data with third parties. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Is MyWrntee really free?

Yes. MyWrntee is completely free with all features unlocked and no advertisements. There is an optional Tip Jar in Settings if you'd like to support development.

More Features

How does the Apple Watch app work?

MyWrntee includes a companion Apple Watch app that syncs your warranties via iCloud. On your watch you can:

  • View a dashboard of active, expiring, and expired warranty counts
  • Browse all warranties or filter by group
  • See full warranty details including dates, price, location, and notes
The watch app is read-only — add and edit warranties on your iPhone.

Important: iCloud Sync must be enabled in the iPhone app (Settings > iCloud) for your warranties to appear on Apple Watch.

What is "Tap to Assign" for receipt scanning?

After scanning a receipt, MyWrntee shows you all the extracted text line by line. Tap any line to assign it to a field like Store Name, Purchase Date, Price, or Notes. This gives you full control over what gets filled in, rather than relying on automatic detection alone.

How do I set up expiry reminders?

Go to Settings > Notifications and choose your reminder intervals: 30 days, 14 days, 7 days, or 1 day before expiry. The app will prompt you to allow notifications if you haven't already. Reminders are sent at 9:00 AM on each selected day.

How do I add home screen widgets?

Long-press on your home screen and tap the + button in the top corner. Search for MyWrntee and choose from small, medium, or large widgets. Widgets show your upcoming warranty expirations at a glance.

Can I use Siri or Spotlight to find warranties?

Yes. Your warranty items are automatically indexed in Spotlight — just swipe down on your home screen and search for any product name. You can also ask Siri to show your expiring warranties or add a new warranty using App Shortcuts.

How do I share warranty details?

Open any warranty item and tap the Share button in the top right. MyWrntee creates a beautifully formatted warranty card (PNG image) that includes all details and attached photos. Share it via Messages, Mail, AirDrop, or any other app.

How does address autocomplete work?

When adding or editing an item, start typing in the Purchase Location field. MyWrntee suggests matching addresses automatically using Apple Maps. Tap a suggestion to fill in the full address. You can also tap any saved location in item details to open it in Apple Maps.

How do sub-groups work?

When creating or editing a group, you can assign it a parent group. This lets you build nested organization like Home > Kitchen or Office > Laptops. Items in sub-groups are also visible when browsing the parent group.

How do I set a lifetime warranty?

When adding or editing an item, tap the Life button in the Warranty Period section. This marks the warranty as lasting forever — it will never show as expired. You can also choose from preset periods: 1 year, 2 years, 3 years, or 5 years.

How do I sort and filter my items?

On the Items tab, use the All / Active / Expired filter at the top to narrow your list. Tap the sort icon (arrows) in the toolbar to sort by purchase date, expiry date, name, or price. Tap the same option again to reverse the order.

What are Quick Actions?

Long-press (or 3D Touch) the MyWrntee app icon on your home screen to see shortcuts: Add Item opens the new item form directly, and Expiring Soon jumps to your dashboard so you can see what needs attention.

What are Warranty Insights?

The Warranty Insights card appears on your Home dashboard once you have 2 or more items. It shows your average warranty length and cost per month of protection, helping you understand the value of your warranty coverage.
